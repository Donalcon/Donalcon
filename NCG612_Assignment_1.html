The Coombes & Bond algorithm, conceived in 2008, utilizes a multidirectional-flow-based approach. Areas are clustered such that; majority of people that work in the area also reside in the area; and the majority of workers that live in the area also work in the area. Clusters are created through bottom-up iterative reassignment, joining together electoral areas, satisfying criteria based on self-containment and size. Crucially, there is a contiguity constraint applied to the algorithm, meaning clusters must be adjoined and not split into parcels of land. Self-containment is the main driving force behind the algorithm. Self-containment is determined by calculating the internal, supply and demand flows.

In the application exercises, the chosen values for the parameters SCmin and SCtarget ranged from 0.4 to 0.5 and 0.5 to 0.9 respectively. Such high values for SCtarget are needed in countries where large areas show low population density such as; Canada, Russia or Australia. However, lower values suffice for municipalities such as county Dublin or even Brindisi. Logically, to ensure that identified functional areas truly capture labour market links, the self-containment parameter should be no less than 0.5
